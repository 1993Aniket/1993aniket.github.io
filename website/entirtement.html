Entertainment

Malta India Film Festival 2018

The PR Objectives

  • The announcement of Malta India Film Festival 2018
  • Promote Malta as a tourist destination
  • The announcement of the brand ambassador ‘Vidya Balan’
  • The announcement of other jury members
  • Mr Engelbert Grech, Director of Malta India Film Academy
  • Mr Vijay Krishna Acharya, Indian Film Director
  • Highlight the incentives offered by the Malta Government to Bollywood industry
  • EUR 200,000 to winning movies (1 Maltese & 1 Indian)

The PR Strategies

  • Press Conference to announce the launch of the first edition of Malta India Film festival 2018
  • The unveiling of a life-size logo of the Malta India Film Festival 2018 at the press conference, giving a photo-opportunity to all media
  • One-on-one interactions with key media before and after the press conference with dignitaries for large format stories:
    • Mr Konrad Mizzi, Tourism Minister, Malta
    • Mr Engelbert Grech, Director, Malta India Film Academy
    • Mr Vijay Krishna Acharya and Mrs Vidya Balan
  • Question and answer round at the press conference to address all media queries

The PR Challenges

  • Bandh in Maharashtra by the Maratha groups
  • The event moved from high tea to cocktails
  • Preferred days would have been Monday-Wednesday
  • During the Q&A round, Vidya Balan answered an off-topic question on her latest biopic. Repercussions enclosed:
  • Story on the biopic picked up by IANS
  • IANS flashed across multiple platforms
  • IANS headline read mentioned the biopic and the article was a mix of the biopic and Malta India Film Festival India
  • Vidya Balan should have refrained from answering the question

The Results

  • Large Format Stories across CAT A publications like Economic Times, Business India, Hindustan Times and Outlook Traveller
  • Media attended the event : 60
  • Total results garnered:
    • Clips – 171
    • PR Value: INR 4,59,29,430
